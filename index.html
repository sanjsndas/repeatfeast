Cooking Feedback & Tips

Perfecting Your Dishes

Always taste as you cook! Adjust seasoning gradually - you can always add more but can't take it out.

Let meat rest before slicing to keep juices in. For most meats, 5-10 minutes makes a big difference.

Don't overcrowd pans - food steams instead of browns when too crowded.

Scaling Recipes

When doubling recipes, increase spices by 1.5x at first, then adjust to taste.

For baking, it's often better to make multiple batches rather than one huge batch.

Big batch cooking? Invest in a kitchen scale for precise measurements.

Crowd-Pleasing Secrets

Offer a variety of textures (something crunchy, something creamy) in meals.

Include at least one familiar element in new dishes to make them approachable.

For potlucks, bring serving utensils and label dishes with ingredients for those with allergies.
